C'mon Google

April 18, 2015

So Google, powerhungry as ever, is trying to patent the idea of an adaptive robotic personality. It would be fine for Google to be patenting a specific robot implementation they were developing, but they've patented the entire idea of an adaptive robot with a personality. The entire document bleeds generality.

This image (taken straight out of the patent's pages) gets to the heart of my issues.

The full patent can be read here.

And it's not like they're the first people to think of this. One of their more interesting, er, "ideas" is for a robot that takes its personality from some phone contact the user defined. The particularly eerie example the patent uses: "Adoption of personality, or some personification attributes, could be more direct, such as a simple user command to adopt a character by name: Be mom... The robot would interact with the cloud to pull sufficient information regarding the new persona to thereby recreate a simulacrum for the robot." Sounds like Google watched Season 2, Episode 2 of Black Mirror and decided that that was a good idea. And then they patented it! The nerve.
